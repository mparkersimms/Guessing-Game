About Me!

Biography

My name is Matthew Simms.
A little about myself: I'm currently in the U.S.Army as CH-47F Instructor Pilot. It's taken me a while to get here, and I'm so proud of what I've been able to accomplish.
My hobbies include: playing and recording music- (mainly acoustic guitar, but I do enjoy piano/keyboard and playing the drums); riding motorcycles - both road bikes, and dirt bikes; mountain biking; climbing; hiking; any board sport: snowboarding, and skateboarding are my favorites.

Education History

I graduated highschool from Lahainaluna High School in Lahaina, HI in 2006. Imediately afterward I attended the University of Hawaii at Manoa in Honolulu, HI for one semester before dropping out. I've attended a couple of other colleges over the last 10 years or so to include Pikes Peak Community College and Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University. I attended the U.S. Army Ranger School, and earned the coveted "Ranger Tab". I also attended and graduated from the U.S. Army Flight School earning my Aviator Wings. I have not completed a two or four year degree program yet, but I am currently enrolled in CodeFellows Coding Bootcamp to become a Software Developer.

Job Experience

My very first job was at a Steak-n-Shake as a dishwasher when I was 15 years old. I worked at a number of different restaurants growing up as well as a few different retail stores.
Some notable companies I have worked for are: Dick's Sporting Goods, Quicksilver, Birkenstock, Coldstone Creamery, Moe's Burritos, Logan's Roadhouse.
I worked for APX Alarms (now VIVINT), an alarm company installing residential alarm systems for a few years before joining the military.
I joined enlisted into the U.S.Army as a 68W Combat Medic in 2010. I spent three years as a Medic before I was accepted into the U.S.Army Flight School in Fort Rucker, AL. I became a CH-47F Helicopter Pilot in 2015, and for the past six years I have been a Pilot, then Pilot-in-Command, then finally an Instructor Pilot in the U.S.Army.
